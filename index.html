Networks of Knowledge, Strength, and Resilience

Educating. Advocating. Empowering.

Mission Statement

Neuro-Networks is dedicated to educating youth, raising awareness and advocacy for brachial plexus injury research, and empowering individuals with brachial plexus injuries to live without limits through education, community, and representation.

We believe that awareness is transformative. When students are informed about neurological injuries, stigma decreases, inclusion strengthens, and leadership grows. Through structured education programs, partnerships, fundraising initiatives, and storytelling platforms, Neuro-Networks builds communities rooted in knowledge, empathy, and resilience.

“Networks of knowledge, strength, and resilience.”

Why Does This Matter?

Brachial plexus injuries are significantly underrepresented in public education and research awareness. Many affected youth face stigma, misunderstanding, and limited peer support. Without accessible information and representation, individuals living with brachial plexus injuries often navigate their journeys in isolation.

Neuro-Networks bridges the gap between medical knowledge and youth understanding through education, advocacy, and empowerment. By making complex medical information accessible and fostering community dialogue, we create inclusive spaces where difference is understood rather than overlooked.

The Reality of Brachial Plexus Injury

The brachial plexus is a network of nerves originating from the spinal cord that controls movement and sensation in the shoulder, arm, and hand. Injury to this network can result in weakness, paralysis, limited mobility, chronic pain, and long-term functional challenges.

1–3 per 1,000 births

Obstetric brachial plexus injuries occur in approximately 1–3 out of every 1,000 live births in the United States.

20–30% Require Surgery

A significant percentage of severe cases do not fully recover spontaneously and require surgical intervention.

Long-Term Impact

Thousands of individuals live with lasting physical and psychosocial effects due to limited awareness and accessibility.

Despite these numbers, funding disparities and awareness gaps persist. Neuro-Networks advocates for equitable research attention and inclusive representation within youth communities.

Core Pillars of Neuro-Networks

Educate

Neuro-Networks educates youth about brachial plexus injuries through school presentations, digital resources, and youth-friendly educational content. We aim to increase understanding, reduce stigma, and make medical information accessible to students. Knowledge builds empathy, awareness, and inclusion.

Advocate

Neuro-Networks advocates for greater awareness, research, and support for brachial plexus injuries. Through campaigns, partnerships, and fundraising initiatives, we amplify voices and push for better understanding and representation. Advocacy connects youth, researchers, and communities.

Empower

Neuro-Networks empowers individuals with brachial plexus injuries to live confidently and without limits. Through mentorship, storytelling, and community-building, we highlight resilience and celebrate diverse abilities. Representation creates confidence, and confidence creates change.
