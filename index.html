Our Mission

Neuro-Networks Foundation is dedicated to educating youth, advancing awareness and advocacy for brachial plexus injury research, and empowering individuals living with brachial plexus injuries to thrive without limits. Through structured education programs, community-building initiatives, and research-focused fundraising, we bridge the gap between complex medical knowledge and youth understanding. Our mission is rooted in representation, inclusion, and leadership.

Why This Matters

Brachial plexus injuries (BPI) affect the network of nerves that control movement and sensation in the shoulder, arm, and hand. These injuries can occur at birth (Obstetric Brachial Plexus Injury) or later due to trauma.

1–3 per 1,000

Babies are affected by obstetric brachial plexus injuries annually in the U.S.

Up to 30%

Of severe BPI cases require surgical intervention.

Thousands

Live with long-term physical and social impacts due to limited awareness and representation.

Our Three Pillars

Educate

We provide youth-centered educational programming, accessible medical breakdowns, school presentations, and digital resources that demystify brachial plexus injuries and reduce stigma.

Advocate

We amplify voices, support research institutions, and build partnerships that increase awareness and funding for brachial plexus injury research.

Empower

We foster mentorship, storytelling, and representation initiatives that ensure individuals with brachial plexus injuries are seen, supported, and celebrated.
