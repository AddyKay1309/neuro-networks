Aadhya Kumar — Founder

Hi! I’m Aadhya Kumar, a junior at the International Academy Okma Campus and the founder of Neuro-Networks. I started this organization to raise awareness about brachial plexus injuries, support research and advocacy, and empower young people living with disabilities.

Growing up with a brachial plexus injury shaped how I see resilience, community, and possibility—and I wanted to create a platform that helps others feel seen, supported, and limitless.

In my free time, I practice Taekwondo, participate in Model United Nations as a Head Delegate, and serve as a board member of my school’s Pre-Med Club where I give human anatomy lectures. I am also heavily involved in the Indian American Student Association. I love learning how leadership and medicine intersect to create real-world impact.

Built Differently

Upload Book Cover Here

Built Differently: Growing Up With a Brachial Plexus Injury is a memoir authored by Aadhya Kumar that shares the wins, challenges, and realities of growing up with a disability.

Writing this book expanded my advocacy beyond storytelling. As I shared my experiences, I realized how little awareness there is about brachial plexus injuries and how many young people feel isolated or misunderstood. That realization led to the creation of Neuro-Networks.

Aarya Verma — Co-Founder

Hi, I'm Aarya Verma, a junior at the International Academy Okma Campus. As Co-founder of Neuro-Networks, my goal is to expand access to medical resources while supporting underserved communities and individuals with medical disabilities.

I am deeply passionate about the intersections between biomedical sciences and technology and aim to support brachial plexus injury research and technological advancements toward treatment through fundraising and advocacy.

I serve as a Scouting Lead and Impact Presenter on an FRC Robotics Team, Global Medical Squads Fundraising Co-Chair, Debate Captain, and am involved in the Indian American Student Association and GUIDAS.
