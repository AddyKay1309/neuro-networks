Aadhya Kumar

Founder

Hi! I’m Aadhya Kumar, a junior at the International Academy Okma Campus and the founder of Neuro-Networks. Growing up with a brachial plexus injury shaped how I understand resilience, representation, and possibility. I created Neuro-Networks to raise awareness, support research, and empower young people living with disabilities to feel limitless.

I practice Taekwondo, serve as Head Delegate in Model UN, am a board member of Pre-Med Club where I give human anatomy lectures, and am involved in the Indian American Student Association. I’m passionate about where leadership and medicine intersect to create meaningful change.

Aarya Verma

Co-Founder

Hi, I’m Aarya Verma, a junior at the International Academy Okma Campus. As Co-founder, I aim to expand access to medical resources and advocate for underserved communities affected by medical disabilities.

I serve as a Scouting Lead and Impact Presenter on an FRC Robotics Team, Global Medical Brigades Fundraising Co-Chair, Debate Captain, and am involved in IASA and GUIDAS. I am passionate about biomedical sciences and technology, and I hope to support advancements in brachial plexus injury research through advocacy and fundraising.

Built Differently

Built Differently: Growing Up With a Brachial Plexus Injury is authored by Aadhya Kumar. Through personal stories and reflections, the memoir explores the wins, challenges, and realities of growing up with a disability. Writing this book expanded Neuro-Networks from storytelling into advocacy, education, and action.
